E-dub-lal-mah

Woolley cleared away the sand which filled the rooms of the building. As his team dug deeper and deeper they revealed arched doorways and courtyards.

This is an inscription found in the courtyard of E-dub-lal-mah. It tells us that the building was dedicated to the moon god Nanna by a king named Amar-Su'en just before 2000 B.C.

E-dub-lal-mah was a gateway to the ziggurat and also a shrine and a courtroom.
