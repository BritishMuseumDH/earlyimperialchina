E-nun-mah

Woolley had to work out a puzzle. All Mesopotamian temples were given a name. Bricks in the floor of this building were stamped with the name E-hur-sag. However, bricks in the walls were stamped with the name E-nun-mah. Which was right?

Woolley worked out that the a nearby building was E-hur-sag and that some spare bricks from it had been used to make a floor in E-nun-mah.

Under one of the floors of E-nun-mah Woolley discovered a hoard of jewellery. Was it a temple offering, or had somebody buried it for some other reason?
