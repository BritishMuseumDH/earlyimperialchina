Trench A: The 'gold trench'

Woolley had chosen to dig trench A because he hoped that its stratigraphy would help him discover the date of a nearby building. It was an exciting moment when his workers began to uncover pots, metal tools and jewellery of carnelian, lapis lazuli and gold.

The jewellery reminded Woolley of objects which dated to around 600 B.C. It was not until long after that he realised that the jewellery was almost two thousand years older!
