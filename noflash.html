The empire founded by Qin Shi Huangdi in 221 BC became the longest surviving empire in the world to date. The early imperial period saw great advances in technology, arts and communications within China. By the beginning of the Yuan dynasty in AD 1279, China was arguably the most advanced country in the world.

www.earlyimperialchina.co.uk covers the period from the unification of the Chinese Empire until the Mongol invasion. This time saw the invention of gunpowder, the introduction of Buddhism into China and a zenith in porcelain production.
