Buddha comes in many different forms and deals with things such as truth and kindness.Buddhism was introduced by Siddhartha Gautama, who had been born into an Indian royal family in the 6th century B.C. He became Buddha when he gave up his life to find Enlightenment after he saw suffering and poverty. Buddhism found its way into China along the Silk Road. The first record of Buddhism in China is in A.D. 65. Texts about Buddhist beliefs were introduced into China and translated in the 5th and 6th centuries A.D.Buddhism was most popular in the Tang dynasty, despite Buddhist monks and nuns suffering persecution in A.D. 845.
In China believers followed traditional Buddhist ideals including meditation; rebirths eventually leading to Nirvana and having to follow the sacred rules.
To fit in with existing Confucian beliefs a change was made to the relationship between husbands and wives. Instead of respecting each other it became rephrased so a husband controls his wife and a wife respects her husband. Worship would take place in temples or places like the Cave of the Thousand Buddhas. Followers would make offerings and pray to a particular image for guidance or help. After death a believer is reincarnated until such time as they have earned the right to be in Nirvana.
