This tile shows Laozi, who was associated with Daoism. Laozi is thought to have lived in the 6th century BC. His beliefs on life were recorded in a book called Daode Jing. He explained that in order to exist without trouble, life must balance with nature and the rest of the world. This is the idea of yin-yang where positive and negative forces work together to balance nature. Later these ideas developed into a religion with many gods being worshipped. The most well known were The Eight Immortals.

Followers of Daoism tried to be at one with their spirit. To achieve this they chose their diet carefully and used breathing exercises to improve their concentration. They tried not to harm anything too. Worship often took place on mountains because the high peaks were believed to reach the spirit world and they were removed from the hurried life of towns. Followers would be rewarded with immortality in the afterlife if they had done their best in life. This supported the belief that the dead could be contacted and offer a believer protection in their life on Earth.
