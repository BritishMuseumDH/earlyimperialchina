This rubbing shows Confucius, who lived during the 6th and early 5th centuries BC. He travelled around different Chinese kingdoms teaching ideas on how people should behave in society. After his death his beliefs became popular. In the Han dynasty they were written down by his followers in a book called The Analects. Believers learned how to respect authority, be moderate and have high levels of morality. Respect of authority depended on the different types of relationships, e.g. emperor and subjects, husband or wife or father and son (known as filial piety).

Filial piety was important because it was the base for morality. Morality came from education and following good examples in life. Moderation came from respecting authority and having high morals. Confucianism also included ancient beliefs in spirits, rivers and the natural elements. Temples and family altars were some places of worship. Descendants of the dead made offerings to their ancestors hoping that they would bring them good fortune and protection. In return this ensured that the dead had a fruitful afterlife.
