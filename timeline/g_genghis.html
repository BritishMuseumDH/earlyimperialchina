Genghis Khan invades the north of China.

Genghis Khan was a brilliant military leader who united the Mongol society. In the Mongol army's first invasion across North China they left around 90 cities totally destroyed. They sacked Beijing in 1215 and it burned for more than a month.

By Genghis' death in 1227, he ruled a large area of land from the Pacific Ocean to the Caspian Sea.

The Mongol conquest continued with Genghis' son and then his grandson, Kubilai, who in 1279 became Emperor of China.
